Development

Google's Dictionary defines development as "a specified state of growth or advancement."
But what does that mean for international relations?

Well, coming back to our puzzle, poorer countries tend to have:
  • Less material wealth and income
    • <\would be nice to fade this in somehow on hover or change up>
    • Between two nations, people in the less developed one tend to make less money from their employment and own less expensive goods.
  • Less equitable distribution of wealth
    • Between two nations, the less developed one tends to have a smaller proprtion of people making much more money than the rest of the population (commonly referred to as the "1%" or "elite").
  • Continued migration from rural to urban areas
    • Between two nations, the less developed one tends to have more people moving to cities from smaller towns or villages since there are opportunities to earn more and receive better access to critical servicesâ€”like education and healthcareâ€”in the cities.
  • Lack of domestic investment in services and infrastructure
    • Between two nations, the less developed one tends to not have as much government money spent on the essentials, such as education, healthcare, and transport. Following the previous bullet point's logic, the expenditures tend to go toward cities first, explaining better access to critical services in these areas.
  • Often, a younger and rapidly growing population
    • Reduced access to family planning resources, in conjunction with lower life expectancies, results in a population that tends to be younger and grow quickly.
